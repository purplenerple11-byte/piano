One octave, every name. Print it, or keep it open beside the keys.

Finding anything, fast

The two seams with no black key between them are E–F and B–C. Everything that goes wrong on this keyboard goes wrong at one of those two seams.

The rule, not the table Sharp = one key to the right. Flat = one key to the left. Applied to any note, including white ones: E♯ is the key right of E, which is F. The table above is just this rule run five times — learn the rule and you can regenerate the table, which is why the rule is the thing worth memorising.
